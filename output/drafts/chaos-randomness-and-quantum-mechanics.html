Chaos, Randomness, and Quantum Mechanics

Posted on September 27, 2019 in physics

A system is said to be chaotic if it is highly sensitive to initial condition. Double pendulum is a classical example. Very little uncertainty or error in the initial condition gets amplified exponentially to be as large as the system size itself. A dynamical variable which is governed by such chaotic non linear dynamics looks very 'random'. We are going to explore more on this on this post.

Let's suppose we have a variable x and a prescribed rule of evolution of this variable which goes as follows

$$x_1 = f(x_0) = 2x mod 1 $$
